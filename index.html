Selected Listening

This couldn't be my website without some music. Here are a few albums I love and think you should check out.

Malibu

On his sophmore album, Anderson.Paak utilizes a wide range of sounds, ranging from jazz to funk to rap to neo-soul to RnB, to produce a fabulous album that is the perfect summer record. My all-time favorite album.

  • Listen

Soul Station

Called "the middleweight champion of the tenor saxophone," Hank Mobley's style is defined by its melodicism. With an amazing band, this record shows Mobley at the top of his game, with his signature bluesy style taking center stage on each track.A masterpiece of hard bop.

  • Listen

Live in Japan

An often unsung hero in BB King's catalogue, Live in Japan allows BB to flex his muscles on longer, more jam-focused renditions of hit tunes, showing everyone why he is the King of the Blues.

  • Listen

A Song For Every Moon

A compilation of songs, written as a challenge to write and record one song each month, Bruno Major's first album is a relaxing mix of RnB, indie, and folk, with each song creating its own space and mood.

  • Listen

The Pretender

Jackson Browne's impecible (and still-relevant) lyricism is on full-display here. Couple that with a excellent variety of tones and subjects and you have a must-listen for any fan of singer-songwriters.

  • Listen

Back to Black

There are very few records I would consider perfect, but Back to Black is one of them. A powerfully sad look into the life of an artist taken in her prime.

  • Listen

Somewhere Under Wonderland

A great record from an early-90s alt-rock staple showing that, even after they left the spotlight, Counting Crows is a solid group with excellent songwriting combined with that quintessential alt-rock feel mixed with some folkier numbers.

  • Listen

Otis Blue

Otis Redding, the undisputed King of Soul, is in peak form here, running the gambit from slow ballads to hard-hitting blues and RnB, with a voice unlike anything that came before or has come since.

  • Listen

Freudian

Daniel Caesar's first record is as sensual and romantic as they come, with each track creating a chilled out, loving mood. One of my favorite voices in modern RnB.

  • Listen

How Long

Ariel Posen is one of my favorite guitar players around today, and he proves that he's a killer singer-songwriter to boot. This selection is a great record and a sign that you should keep your eye on what he does next.

  • Listen

Continuum

John Mayer is one of the last (maybe the last) great guitar heroes and this is the album that cemented his status as not just a pop singer, but a serious blues player. His songwriting is excellent and his guitar playing is superb.

  • Listen

Waiting For Columbus

An excellent "live" record from one of the unsung heroes in Southern rock. With an electricly charged setlist, Little Feat burns through each track in amazing fashion.

  • Listen
